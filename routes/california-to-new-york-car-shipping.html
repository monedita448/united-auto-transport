Shipping a Car Coast to Coast

California to New York is one of the longest and most heavily traveled routes in the country, which works in your favor: high carrier volume on this lane usually means faster pickup windows than shorter, less-traveled routes. Common origin points we dispatch from include Los Angeles, San Diego, Sacramento, and the Bay Area; common New York drop-off points include New York City, Buffalo, Albany, and Rochester.


This route crosses several climate zones and mountain passes (the Rockies and, depending on the carrier's path, the Sierra Nevada), which is one reason we favor carriers experienced on this specific lane, particularly outside the summer months. If your vehicle is a higher-value or low-clearance model, enclosed transport is worth discussing for this route given the distance and weather variability.


Pricing on this lane depends on vehicle size, running condition, time of year, and whether you choose open or enclosed carrier — a quick call gets you a real number instead of a generic estimate.
